03 Long bio

~180 words

For speaker pages, program books, and press

Jason Nellis has spent more than two decades working at the point where media, product, and creator culture collide — not as an observer, but as someone who has built there.

He began his career at Hulu during the early years of streaming, where as a Content Partner Manager he developed content strategy for accounts ranging from the White House to NBCUniversal and Comcast. He learned early that how content is packaged is as strategically significant as the content itself.

He went on to co-found Packagd, a startup built on the thesis that influencer content was generating more value than creators were capturing. Packagd was acquired by Meta in 2019, where the team built and launched Facebook Live Shopping, a rapid-response gift certificate program for small businesses during the pandemic, and Super — a creator monetization platform that powered VidCon's weekly interview series throughout COVID-19.

Jason has served as Chief Product Officer and Interim CEO of SuperBam, an IP and video rights management company, and currently leads strategy across two active ventures. He has guest-lectured at Northwestern, Howard, and Georgetown, hosted the podcast Building Value, and spoken on panels across Europe. He is based near Cannes, France.

05 AV / technical requirements

What to plan for

Standard requirements

  • Projection screen or large display (16:9 preferred)
  • Presenter remote / clicker
  • Slide format: Keynote or PowerPoint
  • Microphone: lavalier preferred, handheld acceptable
  • Confidence monitor or stage display for speaker notes (preferred, not required)

Remote / virtual

Available for virtual and hybrid formats. Stable broadband connection provided from France (CET timezone). Please factor time zone into scheduling for North American engagements.

Language

For audiences where English is not the primary language, a professional interpreter is required. Please confirm language needs at least two weeks in advance.
