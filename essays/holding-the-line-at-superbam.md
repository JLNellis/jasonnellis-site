*Chief Product Officer → Interim CEO, SuperBam, October 2024–2025*

SuperBam is a Los Angeles-based intellectual property and video rights management company that works with content creators to find, claim, and recover revenue from unauthorized use of their content. When platforms support it, the model reroutes ad revenue from infringing videos back to the creator. When they don't, SuperBam pursues takedowns. It is unglamorous, operationally intensive work that directly protects creators' livelihoods.

### Situation

I joined in October 2024 as Chief Product Officer with two mandates: accelerate development of new services and build a client-facing dashboard giving content owners visibility into their claims and revenue in real time — work that required standing up an engineering function that didn't yet exist.

Several months in, the CEO underwent emergency surgery and needed an extended recovery. He asked me to step in as Interim CEO while he was out.

### Decision

The decision was mutual. Despite being based in France, I had already been leading significant portions of the team in my CPO role, and my prior experience building and running companies made the extension feel logical rather than dramatic. I said yes.

What I inherited alongside the role was timing: we were at the start of a new year, YouTube monetization rates had softened, and the first quarter was tracking light on revenue. The team needed someone to captain the ship clearly, not just keep it from sinking.

### What Shipped

Two things mattered most during that period. The first was communication. I kept the team — 30-plus people, with a direct product team of four — informed as transparently as possible about where we were operationally and strategically. In a moment of genuine uncertainty, the most destabilizing thing a leader can do is disappear into the fog. Nobody was left guessing.

The second was momentum. We continued advancing the product roadmap, including meaningful progress on the new dashboard for content owners. We developed a series of important relationship foundations on the sales and BD side. We held costs down. And critically: no one was laid off. The team stayed intact.

### Result

When the CEO returned, the company was in good shape. The team was whole, costs were managed, the product had moved forward, and new commercial relationships were in development. He chose to go in a different direction with the product team at that point, and we ended our professional relationship.

### What I'd Teach

Interim leadership is a specific discipline that most people underestimate. The instinct when you step into a vacuum is to transform — to prove value through change. That instinct is usually wrong.

The job of an interim leader is continuity and confidence. Your team needs to believe the organization will still be standing when the dust clears. That means being visible, being honest about what you know and don't know, and protecting the people around you from the anxiety that comes with leadership uncertainty.

You don't get credit for holding the line. But without someone holding it, there's no line left to return to.
